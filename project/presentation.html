Spring 2020 Ready to Wear

Description

Ready to WEAR 2020 Applique PROCESS: oganza fabric and dori/chord for the outline were dyed in fluorescent color. Place the artwork in a double layered fabric and madkan (lock the outlines) to avoid fraying of fabric. All the madkan areas are highlighted with a dori outlines. For 3D petals, gums were applied to sturdy 3D effects. And Floral machine embroidered laces were stitched and highlighted with faceted beads (football beads). Feathers were stitched along with the faceted beads at the sleeves border.

2.Wedding Project

Description

I love sewing and it’s a wonderful feeling to be a part of a beautiful bride walking down the aisle and leaving everyone in awe. I’m always fascinated to take up various bridal wear projects for which I do freelancing. It’s such a joy to help reimagine a bride’s dream wedding dress and take it to the next level. I ensure that for every wedding dress, the brides themselves are involved in finalizing their wedding theme, color palette, outfit design, designing wedding invitation cards, wedding decor, etc. And with each and every project, I learn something new about both them and myself. This journey has helped me grow in my career as well.

3.Bridal Hair Accessory Project

Description

This project centres around the theme of boldness and elegance that women embody. It was inspired by the head gears, craft technique, flora and fauna of the Tangkhul Naga tribes of Manipur. Handicraft is very integral to the culture and lifestyle of the Tangkhul Nagas. The people are artistic and imaginative. Different designs and styles of the original head gears were imitated from the birds and animals that the ancestors associate with. I adopted some of these designs by using materials that are sustainable which includes bamboo, cane stick, beads, feathers, woollen threads, etc for all the products.
